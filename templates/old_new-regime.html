Old vs. New Tax Regime: Explained

Understanding the two tax regimes is crucial for making an informed choice. The main difference lies in the tax slabs and the availability of deductions and exemptions.

Key Differences in a Nutshell

Feature Old Tax Regime New Tax Regime
Deductions & Exemptions Available (e.g., HRA, LTA, 80C, 80D). You can reduce your taxable income. Very limited deductions. Standard Deduction is not available (except for salary and pension income).
Tax Slabs Tax slabs are higher. Tax is calculated after all deductions are applied. Tax slabs are lower and more numerous. Tax is calculated on a much simpler basis.
Choice The default option for many years. You can switch to the new regime. Introduced as an optional regime. You must choose it explicitly.
Applicability Often more beneficial for those with significant investments and expenses to claim as deductions. Generally more beneficial for those with few or no deductions to claim.
