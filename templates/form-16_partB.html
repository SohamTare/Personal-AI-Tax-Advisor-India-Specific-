Form 16 Part B: Decoding Your Salary

Form 16 Part B is a detailed breakdown of your salary components, exemptions, and the deductions you have claimed. It is a key document for calculating your final taxable income.

Key Components of Part B

This part provides a comprehensive view of your income, including all the allowances and benefits.

  • Gross Salary: The total salary paid to you, including all allowances and perquisites before any deductions or exemptions.
  • Exemptions and Allowances: A list of exemptions you have claimed, such as House Rent Allowance (HRA) or Leave Travel Allowance (LTA), which reduce your taxable income.
  • Deductions (Chapter VI-A): A summary of the tax-saving investments and expenses you've declared to your employer (e.g., under Section 80C, 80D).
  • Taxable Income: The final amount of income after subtracting all exemptions and deductions from your gross salary, on which your tax is calculated.

Why is it important?

Part B helps you understand how your final taxable income was arrived at. It is essential for cross-verifying the deductions your employer has claimed on your behalf and for ensuring that your tax return is accurate.
