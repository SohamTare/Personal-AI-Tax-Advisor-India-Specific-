Form 16 Part A: What it Contains

Form 16 Part A is issued by your employer and contains the details of the Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) on your salary. This is a crucial document for filing your Income Tax Return.

Key Components of Part A

Part A provides a summary of all tax-related information between you and your employer for a specific financial year.

  • Employer and Employee Details: This includes your name, address, PAN (Permanent Account Number), and the TAN (Tax Deduction and Collection Account Number) of your employer.
  • Quarterly TDS Summary: A detailed breakdown of the tax deducted by your employer each quarter of the financial year.
  • Tax Deposited: The total amount of tax deposited by your employer with the government on your behalf, along with the date and mode of payment.
  • Assessment Year: The year in which your income is assessed for taxation.

Why is it important?

Part A serves as proof of the tax you have already paid. When you file your tax return, you must cross-verify the TDS details from your Form 16 Part A with the information in your Form 26AS, an online tax statement. This ensures that the tax paid by your employer has been correctly credited to your account.
