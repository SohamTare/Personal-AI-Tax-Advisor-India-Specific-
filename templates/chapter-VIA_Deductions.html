A Simple Guide to Chapter VI-A Deductions

Chapter VI-A of the Income Tax Act provides a list of common deductions that can be claimed by individuals and Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs) to reduce their taxable income.

Popular Sections to Claim Deductions

Here is a breakdown of the most frequently used sections to save tax.

  • Section 80C

    This is the most popular tax-saving section. It allows for a maximum deduction of **₹1.5 lakhs** on a variety of investments and expenses, including:

    • Life Insurance premiums.
    • Provident Fund (EPF/PPF) contributions.
    • Equity Linked Savings Schemes (ELSS).
    • Children's tuition fees.
    • Principal repayment of a home loan.

  • Section 80D

    This section allows for a deduction on health insurance premiums paid. The limits are:

    • Up to **₹25,000** for yourself, your spouse, and dependent children.
    • An additional **₹25,000** for parents (or **₹50,000** if they are senior citizens).

  • Section 80E

    You can claim a deduction on the **entire interest paid** on an education loan taken for yourself, your spouse, or your children. This deduction is available for a maximum of 8 years.

  • Section 80G

    This section allows you to claim a deduction for **donations** made to certain approved charitable funds and institutions. The deduction amount can be 50% or 100% of the donated amount, depending on the recipient.

  • Section 80TTA & 80TTB

    This is for claiming deductions on interest earned from bank deposits:

    • **80TTA:** A deduction of up to **₹10,000** on savings account interest (for individuals and HUFs).
    • **80TTB:** A deduction of up to **₹50,000** on all bank interest (for senior citizens).
